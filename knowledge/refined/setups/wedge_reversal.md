# Setup: Wedge Reversal

## Scope (MANDATORY)
- Market: US Stocks
- Timeframe: Not explicitly specified
- Direction: Counter-Trend
- Style: Reversal

## Market Preconditions
- Market is at:
  - End of a trend, OR
  - Edge of a trading range.
- Momentum shows signs of exhaustion.

## Entry Triggers
- Three pushes (3 Pushes):
  - Successive higher highs (bearish wedge)
  - OR successive lower lows (bullish wedge)
- After third push:
  - Strong reversal signal bar appears.
  - For short: bearish bar closing near its low.
- Entry:
  - Stop order 1 tick beyond signal bar in reversal direction.

## Invalidation / Failure Conditions
- No reversal signal after third push.
- Strong trend bar breaks beyond wedge extreme.
- Post-reversal move lacks follow-through and becomes a flag.

## Risk Management
- Stop loss placed beyond wedge extreme:
  - Highest high (bearish wedge)
  - Lowest low (bullish wedge)

## Profit Taking
- Expect at least:
  - Two-legged correction (TBTL: Ten Bars, Two Legs)
- Initial target:
  - Origin point of wedge.

## Common Mistakes
- Attempting wedge reversal inside a very strong trend (tight channel).
- Entering without a clear signal bar.
